How Our Delivery Flow Works

A structured, 10-stage process covering every phase from site identification through to occupation — adapted for South African delivery realities.

Stage 1

Site Identification & Feasibility

Site selection, title deed review, zoning checks, bulk services availability, and site constraints analysis before committing resources.

Stage 2

Land Use Application

Rezoning, consent use, subdivision, and removal of restrictive conditions — from pre-consultation through public participation to municipal approval.

Stage 3

Concept Design

Client brief, space planning, concept sketches, and basic floor plans to turn the idea into a visual concept.

Stage 4

Site Development Plan

Full site layout including building footprint, parking, access roads, landscaping, and drainage concept for municipal approval.

Stage 5

Detailed Architectural Plans

Floor plans, elevations, sections, roof plans, door & window schedules, and construction details for building plan approval.

Stage 6

Engineering Design

Structural engineering (foundations, slabs, columns), civil engineering (stormwater, sewer, roads), and electrical/mechanical systems design.

Stage 7

Bill of Quantities

Accurate project costing covering excavation, concrete, brickwork, roofing, finishes, electrical, and plumbing for tendering and budgeting.

Stage 8

Tender & Contracting

Issue BoQ and drawings, evaluate contractor bids, appoint contractor, and finalise contract agreements and project timelines.

Stage 9

Construction

Site clearing, earthworks, foundations, superstructure, roofing, finishes, and services installation — supervised by architect, engineer, and project manager.

Stage 10

Inspections & Completion

Municipal inspections, engineer sign-off, Electrical Certificate of Compliance (COC), and final Occupation Certificate for legal occupation.
